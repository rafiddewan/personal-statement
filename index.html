Started From The Bottom And Now We Are Here

I first got my hands on code in grade 11. Learning how to code made me frustrated and I told my dad I'll never pick it up again. My teacher, who taught nutrition class, came to me and asked if I was still taking the computer science course in grade 12. She then asked me if it'd be possible to make a nutrition application for her so she can record her calorie intake progression.

I was so excited about this project that I contacted a friend of mine, and we began to make a nutrition application. It wasn't the best at the time; we created a fully-functional desktop application and a half-completed Android application. I never had this much fun in my life. It instantly got me thinking of majoring in software engineering. The thing I loved about software engineering the most was doing something that will create an impact and help others. That's the reason why I went from hating software engineering, to loving it so much.

4 Months at Innovapost

In the summer of 2019, I worked at Innovapost for four months as a co-op student.

I was quite astonished by the product I was developing. This was transitioning from a local in-house data center to two active remote ones. This helps large enterprises (such as Innovapost) to provide continuous operations for clients despite a bad storm causing an outage occuring in one of them (as the other would be at another location). I did not have any previous experience in cloud development or automation going into this job. I worked in an Agile/Scrum environment using Ansible to automate resources on the Azure Cloud and schedule their tasks with AnsibleTower (such as deleting a VM gracefully).

It was a balanced mix of tasks: architecting, developing and testing my automation scripts. I was also running around between stakeholders to adjust to their requirements accordingly. I'm very thankful for my co-op at Innovapost as I've gained a considerable amount of experience in DevOps.

Shopify is the leading cloud-based commerce platform in the world. I find that my skills in cloud automation and knowledge of Microsoft's best practices with Azure in a large-scale enterprise environment would serve to help the deployment of many massive DevOps products.

Taking my skills to the test and competing with the rest

In August 2018, I competed in a tech entrepreneurship competition, called Sparkfest 2018. I led a team with two of my friends, and we brainstormed ideas of what we should make. We ended up thinking about a creative way for developers to do freelance work. Our product was called HackrTrackr. It was an Airbnb type application where you request someone to help you on a side project or troubleshoot a problem, once they do you have to rate them and give them a review. We won second place at Sparkfest 2018, and I got to meet incredible professionals, one of which was the guest speaker who was Jace Meyer, Shopify's Research and Development Education Lead at the time.

The next competition I took place in was cuHacking 2020. We developed a user-friendly web app called House Hunter. House Hunter optimizes a more straightforward way to find rental listings for students. We web scraped data from Kijiji real-time. Then, we used ML to find the best value for the prices of apartment/house rentals that have a certain number of beds. We prototyped the design of our website using Figma. We then used React.JS and the Material UI framework to implement the frontend. At the end of cuHacking 2020, we won the best UI/UX award.
